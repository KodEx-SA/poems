title: The Voice That Stays
author: Lawliet

I've worn cold like armour before,
trained my eyes to look through people,
practiced silence where warmth used to live,
told myself it was survival.

But every time I tried to act heartless a small voice inside me whispers, "This isn't who you are."

It doesn't shout.
It doesn't beg.
It just — reminds me.
Like a candle that refuses the wind.

I've stood at the edge of cruelty
and called it protecting myself.
I've swallowed apologies
and called it being strong.
But that voice never learned the script
I wrote for all my walls.

It knows the version of me
that cried at small, honest things —
a stranger's kindness,
a song that hit too close,
the way some people love
without needing a reason.

So I keep coming back.
Not because the world made it easy,
but because somewhere underneath
all the armour and the ache —

that voice is still right.
